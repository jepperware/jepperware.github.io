    We know that successful adoption of DevOps principles requires buy-in, in particular, from C-level operatives. When we say buy-in, we mean devotion, clarity, vision, acceptance of the DevOps narrative. Time and again we've witnessed great people, entire teams, being shot down by middle manager ITIL jockeys ruling the path to production, and C-types professing Agile in spirit, but ITIL by action. They never see it: Self defeating risk-averseness, turning DevOps into a mirage. Are you a C-type, our deepfelt advise to you is: Adopt Agile completely, forget ITIL, bin the Gant charts, toss out your Prince2 practioners, become a risk-taker, a thought leader, a hero to your subordinates, and by doing so, attract better people, reduce churn, increase velocity, domain knowledge per head, deliver more, ensure the success of your business. Realise that avoiding risk is the greatest risk to success.
